<p><p>Medical helium is a specialized form of helium used in various healthcare applications, primarily in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) machines, where it serves as a coolant for superconducting magnets. It is also utilized in respiratory therapies, particularly for patients with severe asthma or other respiratory conditions, as a component in heliox (a mixture of helium and oxygen) to improve airflow and reduce breathing effort. Additionally, helium is employed in cryogenics, laser technologies, and other medical devices.</p><p>The Medical Helium Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the increasing demand for advanced diagnostic imaging technologies, such as MRI, and the rising prevalence of respiratory diseases. The expansion of healthcare infrastructure in emerging economies and the growing adoption of helium-based therapies are also contributing factors.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of helium recycling technologies to address supply shortages and reduce costs. Additionally, advancements in helium-free MRI systems are being explored, though these are still in the early stages. The market is also witnessing a shift toward sustainable practices, with companies focusing on minimizing helium wastage and improving supply chain efficiency. These factors are expected to shape the future of the Medical Helium Market.</p></p>
