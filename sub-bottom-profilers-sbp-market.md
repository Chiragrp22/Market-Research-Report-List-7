Low frequency SBPs penetrate deeper, making them ideal for geological surveys and assessing larger sediment structures. In contrast, high frequency SBPs offer higher resolution imagery, suitable for detailed investigations of shallow sediments and features. Both types play crucial roles in marine research, engineering, and environmental assessments.</p></p>
