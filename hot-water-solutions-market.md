<p><p>The hot water solutions market can be divided into two main types: centralized and decentralized systems. Centralized systems involve a central unit that generates and distributes hot water to multiple outlets or buildings, ensuring uniform temperature and efficiency. In contrast, decentralized systems provide individual hot water solutions, such as point-of-use heaters, allowing for localized control and on-demand heating. Both systems have unique advantages, with centralized systems often being more efficient for large-scale applications, while decentralized systems offer flexibility and reduced installation costs.</p></p>
