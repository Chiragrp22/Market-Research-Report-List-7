<p><p>The OLED light-emitting layer primarily utilizes blue host materials, categorized into fluorescent and phosphorescent types. Fluorescent materials emit light through rapid transitions between energy levels, providing bright colors but lower efficiency. In contrast, phosphorescent materials harness triplet excitons, enabling longer-lasting light emission and improved efficiency, making them more desirable in modern displays. The market is shaped by ongoing advancements in material quality and performance, driving demand for both types in applications like TVs, smartphones, and lighting technologies.</p></p>
