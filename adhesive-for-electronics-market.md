<p><p>The adhesive for electronics market is divided into two main types: non-electrically conductive and electrically conductive adhesives. Non-electrically conductive adhesives are used for applications where insulation is crucial, such as bonding components without affecting electrical pathways. In contrast, electrically conductive adhesives are designed to facilitate electrical connections between components, often used in circuit assembly and sensors. Each type serves specific purposes, ensuring reliable performance in electronic devices, depending on the required electrical properties and bonding strength.</p></p>
