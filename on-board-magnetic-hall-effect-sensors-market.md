<p><strong>The On-Board Magnetic Hall Effect Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Below 1 Microgauss (Low-Field Sensors)</li><li>1 Microgauss to 10 Gauss (Earth Field Sensors)</li><li>Above 10 Gauss (Bias Magnetic Field Sensors)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>On-Board Magnetic Hall Effect Sensors are categorized into three main types based on their sensitivity to magnetic fields. "Below 1 Microgauss" sensors, or Low-Field Sensors, detect extremely weak magnetic fields, making them ideal for precision applications. "1 Microgauss to 10 Gauss" sensors, or Earth Field Sensors, are designed to measure magnetic fields similar to the Earth's, suitable for geophysical explorations. "Above 10 Gauss" sensors, or Bias Magnetic Field Sensors, can handle stronger magnetic environments, often used in industrial and automotive applications.</p></p>
