<p><p>The industrial lanolin market primarily consists of two types: anhydrous lanolin and hydrous lanolin. Anhydrous lanolin, a waxy, fat-like substance, has a high lipid content, making it ideal for applications requiring moisture retention and emollience, often used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. Hydrous lanolin, containing water, is more hydrophilic and suited for formulations needing additional moisture. Both types serve diverse industries, including personal care, food processing, and pharmaceuticals, with each type offering unique properties for specific applications.</p></p>
