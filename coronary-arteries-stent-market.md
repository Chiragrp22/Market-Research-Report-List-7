<p><p>The coronary artery stent market comprises two primary types: metal and polymer stents. Metal stents, typically made from stainless steel or cobalt-chromium, offer strength and durability, making them widely used for immediate structural support in arteries. Polymer stents, often polymer-coated or biodegradable, provide flexibility and biocompatibility, catering to patients needing reduced inflammation and improved healing. Both types serve essential roles in coronary interventions, addressing varying patient needs and disease complexities in cardiovascular care.</p></p>
